# Understanding market odds

## How prediction markets work

Every market card shows an **implied chance** — the market's current estimate of how likely each outcome is, based on real money traded:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Implied chance">
    - **Yes at 72%** means the market currently implies a 72% chance the event happens
    - The implied chance moves as people trade and new information emerges
    - Buying an outcome pushes its implied chance up; selling pushes it down
    - Markets can have more than two outcomes — the same logic applies to each one
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Your potential returns">
    - When you set up a trade, the ticket shows an **estimated payout if you win**
    - The lower the implied chance when you buy, the higher your estimated payout if you're right
    - The estimate can shift as others trade — the final payout is your pro-rata share of the market's reserve at resolution
    - If your outcome loses, your position is worth nothing at resolution
    - **Markets stay liquid** — you can exit positions at any time at the current market price
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Market dynamics">
    - **Early predictions** often get better prices before consensus forms
    - **Breaking news** can rapidly shift the implied chance
    - **Large trades** move prices more than small ones
    - **Arbitrage** keeps prices roughly in line with real-world probabilities
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# How to make predictions

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="On the webapp">
    **The primary way to trade — app.kash.bot**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Find a market">
        Browse trending markets, categories, or search on [app.kash.bot](https://app.kash.bot). Each card shows the question and the current implied chance.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Pick a side">
        Tap **Yes** or **No** (or the specific outcome in multi-outcome markets). The trade ticket opens.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Set your amount">
        Enter how much USDC to spend. The ticket shows your shares and the **estimated payout if you win** before you confirm.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm">
        Confirm the trade — it executes gaslessly from your Kash wallet, and your position appears in your Portfolio immediately.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Trading from X">
    **Optional: trade by replying on X (Twitter)**

    Kash can turn your X account into a trading interface. This is **opt-in** — enable it under **Settings → Trading bot** on app.kash.bot first. Trades go through **Kash's dedicated trading account** — find the current handle on the [@kash_bot](https://x.com/kash_bot) profile.

    ## Simple format
    Reply to or repost any Kash market post with:
    - **"YES 50"** — put $50 on the event happening
    - **"NO 100"** — put $100 on it not happening
    - **"YES $25"** — dollar sign optional, case doesn't matter

    ## What happens next
    1. Kash confirms your prediction intent
    2. Checks your wallet balance
    3. Executes the trade at the current market price
    4. Replies with your position details

    **Example interaction:**
    ```
    You: "YES 100"
    Kash trading bot: "Bought Yes at 72% implied chance
               Total cost: $100
               Estimated payout if Yes wins: ~$137"
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Market resolution">
    **How outcomes are determined**

    ## Market close
    - Trading stops at the market's deadline
    - Existing positions are locked in
    - The market awaits resolution

    ## Resolution and claiming
    - The market's oracle sets the winning outcome on-chain
    - Winning positions become **claimable in your Portfolio**
    - If a market can't be resolved, it can be cancelled and positions become refundable

    See the [winning guide](/getting-started/guides/winning) for exactly how payouts, claims, and refunds work.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

# Prediction strategies

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Information edge">
    - **Research before predicting** — read news, analyze trends, understand context
    - **Follow reliable sources** — trust verified accounts and expert analysis
    - **Spot market inefficiencies** — find implied chances that don't match reality
    - **Act on breaking news** — be first to capitalize on new information
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Portfolio approach">
    - **Diversify across markets** — don't put all eggs in one basket
    - **Don't trade your entire balance** — keep reserves for better opportunities
    - **Take profits regularly** — lock in gains instead of letting them ride
    - **Size predictions to your confidence level**
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Social signals">
    - **Watch successful predictors** — follow proven track records on the leaderboards
    - **Monitor sentiment shifts** — spot when opinion is changing before the price does
    - **Learn from patterns** — recognize recurring market behaviors
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# Managing your positions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Track your positions">
    - Your **Portfolio** shows every open position, its current value, and live profit and loss
    - Watch how the implied chance moves after your entry
    - Plan your exit — sell early or hold to resolution
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Sell before close">
    - **Lock in profits early** — secure gains before the market closes
    - **Cut losses** — minimize damage when you're clearly wrong
    - Sell from the market page or your Portfolio at the current market price
    - Sells pay a 1% protocol fee plus a 1% AMM fee
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Add to a position">
    - Buy the same outcome again anytime to increase your stake
    - Adding at a different price averages your effective entry
    - No limit on how many trades you can place
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# Pro tips

- **Early markets** often have better prices before consensus forms
- **Breaking news** creates temporary price inefficiencies
- **Start small** while learning market dynamics — fees are 1% on buys, 1% + 1% on sells
- **Join the market chat** — every market has a discussion where traders share their reasoning

<Tip>
Every expert predictor started with their first prediction. Start small, learn the dynamics, and let your edge compound over time!
</Tip>
