# Fees

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What fees does Kash charge?">
    | Action | Fee |
    |---|---|
    | **Buying** outcome shares | 1% protocol fee |
    | **Selling** outcome shares | 1% protocol fee + 1% AMM fee |
    | **Claiming winnings** (redemption) | No fee |
    | **Claiming a refund** (cancelled market) | No fee |
    | **Creating a market** | Creation fee (shown in the creation flow) |
    | **Deposits** | No Kash fee — payment providers charge their own processing fees |
    | **Gas** | Sponsored by Kash (see gas exception below) |

    Fees are charged at the time of the trade — nothing extra is deducted from winning payouts, and there are no subscription, maintenance, or hidden fees.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Fee example">
    ```
    Buy: $100 on Yes
    Protocol fee: 1% = $1.00
    Total cost: $101.00

    Sell later: position worth $150
    Protocol fee (1%) + AMM fee (1%) = $3.00
    You receive: $147.00

    Or hold to resolution and win:
    Claim your payout — no redemption fee
    (mainnet claims may deduct a small gas amount in USDC)
    ```
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does gas sponsorship work?">
    Kash wallets are ERC-4337 smart accounts, and transaction gas is paid by a **paymaster** — so you never need to hold ETH:

    - **Covered:** trades, deposits credited to your account, position management
    - **One exception:** on mainnet, **claiming winnings may deduct a small gas amount in USDC from the payout** — you receive the net USDC. This is the only time gas touches your balance, and it's taken from winnings, never charged separately.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# Trades

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do trades work?">
    1. You confirm a trade on app.kash.bot (or via tweet-trading if you've enabled it)
    2. The trade executes on-chain from your own smart wallet on Base — gas sponsored
    3. Your position and balance update immediately, with a transaction hash you can verify on a block explorer

    The trade ticket always shows the price, fees, and your **estimated payout if you win** before you confirm.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I cancel or modify a trade?">
    Confirmed blockchain transactions are final — there's no reversal mechanism. But you're never locked in:

    - **Sell your position** anytime before the market closes, at the current market price (1% protocol + 1% AMM fee)
    - **Partial sales** work too — reduce your position while keeping some exposure
    - Large positions may face price impact when exiting, since your own sale moves the price
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why did my transaction fail?">
    Common causes:

    - **Insufficient balance** — the amount plus the 1% buy fee exceeds your available USDC
    - **Market closed** — trading stops at the market deadline
    - **Price moved** — the price shifted beyond your slippage tolerance between quote and execution
    - **Rate limits** — a small number of actions (like withdrawals, capped at 5 per hour) are rate-limited

    If a trade fails, nothing is charged — check the market status and your balance, then retry. If it keeps failing, contact support@kash.bot.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# Claims and payouts

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I claim my winnings?">
    When a market resolves and your outcome wins, the position becomes **claimable**:

    1. Open your **Portfolio** on app.kash.bot
    2. Press **Claim** on the resolved position
    3. Your winning shares redeem for a **pro-rata share of the market reserve**, and USDC lands in your wallet

    On mainnet, the claim may deduct a small gas amount in USDC — you receive the net payout. There is no Kash fee on redemption. Cancelled markets work the same way with a **Claim refund** button, refunding each outcome at its price snapshotted at cancellation.

    Full details in the [winning guide](/getting-started/guides/winning).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How much will I receive when I win?">
    Winning shares redeem for a proportional share of the market's USDC reserve — not a fixed amount per share. The **estimated payout if you win** shown on the trade ticket is your best guide; the final number is fixed at resolution based on the reserve and the total supply of winning shares. If multiple outcomes win, they share the reserve.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# Withdrawals

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How long do withdrawals take?">
    Withdrawals are processed automatically (no manual approval) and **typically complete within minutes**:

    1. **Pending** — request validated and queued
    2. **Processing** — executing on-chain
    3. **Confirming** — awaiting blockchain confirmation
    4. **Completed** — funds at your destination address

    Withdrawals to networks other than Base are bridged automatically and take roughly 10–15 minutes. Exchanges may add a few minutes before crediting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What are the withdrawal limits?">
    | Limit | Value |
    |---|---|
    | Minimum per transaction | $1 |
    | Maximum per transaction | $25,000 |
    | Daily maximum | $10,000 |
    | Weekly maximum | $50,000 |
    | Withdrawals per hour | 5 |

    Supported destination networks: Base, Ethereum, Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon. Cross-chain withdrawals may include bridging costs, shown in the quote before you confirm.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# Tracking and problems

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I track my transactions?">
    - **In the app:** your Portfolio history lists every trade, claim, deposit, and withdrawal with live status
    - **On-chain:** every transaction has a hash you can look up on a Base block explorer (basescan.org) — all activity is publicly verifiable and permanent
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I sent funds to the wrong address?">
    Confirmed blockchain transactions cannot be reversed, and funds go to whoever controls the destination address — Kash cannot recover misdirected funds.

    **Prevention:**
    - Copy-paste addresses, never type them
    - Confirm the destination network matches what the recipient supports
    - Send a small test amount first when using a new address
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I report a transaction problem?">
    1. Check the transaction status in the app and on a block explorer first
    2. Contact **support@kash.bot** or use the in-app feedback widget
    3. Include: the transaction hash (if any), your wallet address, what you expected, and what happened

    Screenshots help. Never share your login credentials with anyone — support will never ask for them.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
All confirmed transactions on Kash are final. Verify amounts, addresses, and networks carefully before confirming — completed transactions cannot be reversed.
</Warning>

<Tip>
Kash sponsors gas for your transactions, so you only pay the trading fees: 1% on buys, 1% + 1% on sells, and nothing to claim winnings.
</Tip>
